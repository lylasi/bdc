# My first Chinese New Year

Hey Charles,

Guess what! I'm now celebrating Chinese New Year in Hong Kong. Let me tell you what I did in the past few days.

I'm staying at my friend Ben Lee's home. Two days before Chinese New Year, the Lee family did house cleaning. Mrs Lee told me that no one swept the floor on the first day of Chinese New Year in the past. People believed this would sweep away the good luck for the New Year. Nowadays, not many people still follow this tradition.

On Chinese New Year's Eve, I visited the flower market with Mr Lee and Ben. Mr Lee bought something there. It was a mandarin tree. Look at the photo. The mandarin tree isn't real, but I love it. It looks very cute! Mr Lee told me that the Chinese words for mandarins and luck sound similar. Also, the orange colour of mandarins looks like gold. People like having this plant at home for good luck. Let me tell you a little secret. I've already taken one of the mandarins off the tree. I'm going to bring some luck home!

On the first day of Chinese New Year, the Lee family took me to watch a parade. It was interesting, but the street was too crowded. I liked the lion dance most because it was so amazing. The next night, we watched the fireworks. They were really beautiful, but too loud for me.

The Lee family visited lots of their relatives during Chinese New Year. Ben got many red packets from them.

I didn't think I'd get one because I'm not a member of their family, but I did. What a surprise! I'm rich now!

I'll never forget this wonderful experience! I've made a piece of special red paper for the Lee family. Take a look! It's a poem.

Winter is leaving, spring is here.
I wish everyone a happy New Year!
So much wonderful food to eat.
So many relatives and friends to meet!
May everything that you wish come true.
May you always be happy and healthy too!

See you at school soon.

Yours,
Max

# Where was the wedding ring?

Betty and Billy were a great help to their uncle Eddy on his big day.

It was almost time for the groom Eddy to give the bride Nicole the ring. He asked his brother Henry for the ring. Henry was the best man. He helped Eddy at the wedding. Henry searched in his pockets, but he could not find it.

'I thought the ring was in my pocket, but it's not here,' said Henry.

'Oh no!' Eddy felt terrible.

'Don't worry, Uncle Eddy,' said Betty. 'We can help,' said Billy.

'Thanks, Betty and Billy!' said Eddy. 'It's a diamond ring. Please help me find it.'

'No problem, Uncle Eddy!' Billy and Betty started looking around.

Billy said to Betty, 'Can you see the tall man in striped trousers? He looks very nervous. I think he took the ring.'

'That's Aunt Nicole's father,' said Betty. 'I don't think he took it.'

Betty found a silver ring on the floor under a table. She picked it up and asked, 'Whose ring is this?'

'It's mine!' said a woman in a spotted dress. 'Thank you very much for helping me find it!'

Billy saw a woman with a diamond ring. 'Uncle Eddy,' he said, 'look at that woman in a spotted skirt. She's wearing a diamond ring. Is it yours?'

Eddy looked closely. 'No,' he said, 'mine is different.'

'Oh yes, I remember now!' shouted Henry suddenly. 'Right after Eddy gave me the ring in its box, Mum asked me to help her put on her sweater. I left the box on the chair ...'

'Henry!' said Eddy loudly.

'Sorry, Eddy.'

'I know where it is!' Billy and Betty said together. They ran to the chair and found the box with the ring inside.

At that moment, the wedding song started to play. Thank goodness!

# People we admire

## Safina Lam

Recently, I got the chance to talk with the voice actress Safina Lam. People often call her the daughter of Doraemon because her father was Francis Lam Pou-chuen. He was a famous voice actor and played the role of the robot cat Doraemon.

Ben: How long have you been a voice actress?

Safina: I've been a voice actress since 2003.

Ben: Many people love your father's voice. I'm one of them. Is he the reason you became a voice actress too?

Safina: Yes, he is. He inspired me to become a voice actress. When I was a little girl, my mum and I often went to watch him work in the recording studios.

Ben: What was your father like then?

Safina: He was cheerful and gentle. I was quite naughty sometimes, but he never scolded me or punished me.

I love watching cartoons. I hope I can be a voice actor like Safina and her father when I grow up.

## Fung Kam-hung

Fung Kam-hung is an amazing person. He lost his left leg in a traffic accident in 1979, but he never gave up. He has been a runner since 2007. He finished his first 10-kilometre running race that year.

In 2011, Fung completed the 100-kilometre Oxfam Trailwalker with his wife and two other team members.

Fung and his caring wife have been married for more than 30 years. He met her after the accident. She is a nurse. She took care of him in the hospital then.

In 2015, Fung's team won the 250-kilometre desert race in Chile. It was Fung's first race outside Hong Kong. He did hard training every week to prepare for the desert race. He is such a patient person. That is why I admire him. He is also a helpful person. He raises money for charity by running. He is a role model for me!

## My first camping trip

I went camping with my family three years ago. There were so many animals at the campsite. My little brother was very frightened when he saw a wild pig. I was not worried because it was far away. # MouseMan's Hole

Welcome to my blog! I'm MouseMan. I feel like a mouse going down a hole when I travel on the MTR every day.

## Be considerate on the MTR, people!

Yesterday, I was at the front of a long queue on the platform in Central Station. Suddenly, a man jumped the queue and stood in front of me. Others in the queue weren't happy. I felt it was my duty to say something. 'Excuse me,' I said. 'You ought to go to the back of the queue.' He looked at me and then started watching his video again.

## BPolite

Right! We ought to queue up.

Today, in Mong Kok, there were two women at the front of the queue. The train came. They took one step inside and stopped. They stood near the doors like trees and made everyone go around them. People, please learn to move all the way into the train. We ought not to block the doors.

## MumMum

Something very nice happened to me on the MTR today. Passengers rushed to get onto the train once it arrived in Admiralty. I was red in the face and wanted to shout at them ... Then they saw my friend and me with our babies in prams. They stopped, backed up and let us get off the train. That was really nice! We all ought to let passengers exit first.
